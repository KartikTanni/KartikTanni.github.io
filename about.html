ABOUT

Magnovite

CHRIST (Deemed to be University) Faculty of Engineering and School of Architecture will conduct an annual techno cultural fest, with a multitude of events lined up in diverse Technical and Cultural categories. Pre-Fest talks by some eminent personalities and very interesting workshops will ensure that students match up to the level that this Fest will demand. Magnovite Season 7 is a fest that shows that life is a blend of Art and Technology, it is the FUSION of the two that makes life and the fest the indomitable phenomenon that it is.

  • Workshop Day 1

    February 27th, 2018

    Prior to Magnovite, a series of workshops are organised to facilitate greater understanding and curiosity in a variety of fields both, Technical and Non-Technical. On Day 1, workshop participants will gain an introductory understanding and begin learning practical implementations of their chosen area.

  • Workshop Day 2

    February 28th, 2018

    On Day 2, workshop participants continue to delve deeper and explore new horizons under the guidance of their esteemed workshop conductors. Participants will apply their knowledge to real life situations and learn first-hand the intricacies of the industry.

  • Magnovite Day 1

    March 1st, 2018

    Magnovite Season 7 launches into action on 1st March, at 9:00 am with a spectacular inauguration ceremony graced by the presence of the esteemed Mr. Haja Mohideen, President and CEO of Rocheston. The high spirits from the vivacious ceremony are carried forward through the start of several major Technical and Non-Technical Events. The day comes to a close with the enthralling performances of over 300 dancers, participating in the crown jewel of Magnovite – Nritta.

  • Magnovite Day 2

    March 2nd, 2018

    On Day 2, Christites from across our three campuses can join the merriment as events open up to internal participation. Meanwhile, challenging events enter their second day and exciting, new events open their doors to participants. Join hands in encouraging our budding architects, engineers and designers as they attempt to enter the Limca Book of Records by creating the largest collage in the country themed, “A Silhouette of Bengaluru”. Once again, the day concludes with a thrilling performance by Mr. Stephen Devassy, a magician on the piano keys.

  • Magnovite Day 3

    March 3rd, 2018

    The day commences with an interesting panel discussion on the “Next Generation Pedagogy” which presents a platform that allows students to get exposure at an international level about the various fields in which they can pursue their careers. Alas, all good things must come to an end and Magnovite concludes with a Valedictory Ceremony for all winners and organisers.
